Step 4: Safety Certification

The FMCSA requires all applicants to certify their knowledge and compliance with safety regulations.

APPLICANT CERTIFICATION: I certify that I am familiar with the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Regulations (FMCSRs) and/or the Hazardous Materials Regulations (HMRs). I certify that I have in place a system for the following:

  • Safe vehicle operation and maintenance.
  • Driver qualification and drug/alcohol testing.
  • Hours of service monitoring (Logbooks/ELDs).
  • Meeting financial responsibility (insurance) requirements.

Step 5: Business Affiliations

Disclose any relationships with other FMCSA-regulated entities from the past three years.

Includes stock ownership, loans, management positions, or familial relationships with owners/managers of other companies.

Check current ratings on the [FMCSA SAFER System](https://safer.fmcsa.dot.gov/CompanySnapshot.aspx).

Step 6: Household Goods Attestations

As a mover or HHG broker, you must certify your ability to provide specialized services and dispute arbitration.

Applicant certifies they are fit, willing, and able to provide specialized HHG services, possess protective equipment/trained operators, and will offer **arbitration** for loss/damage disputes.

Applicant certifies service is consistent with the public interest and the transportation policy of 49 USC 13101.

Applicant is fit and able to provide HHG brokerage operations and comply with all pertinent statutory and regulatory requirements.
